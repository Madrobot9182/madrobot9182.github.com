A Message to the UN
Sustainable Prosperity for the Future Generations

The problem

As globalization continues to expand, the world we live in is currently struggling to keep up. Climate change ravages on, water levels are raising, and dangerous natural disasters are created. With about every country in the world signing the Paris agreement, the majority of them are not even close to the goals. The US is even leaving the agreement. Wealth inequality is now the highest it has ever been, with the top 1% holding 44% of the world's wealth. Large companies are exploiting free trade and lesser developed areas with cheap labor and relaxed regulations. Using their influence with the WTO to bypass local laws and regulations. Little is currently being done to combat climate change as forests are mowed down and our oceans are being filled with garbage. Countries and companies are instead of throwing the environment away in exchange for greater profits. The conclusion is that our current way of life cannot continue forever. Eventually, we will run out of resources, and when that happens it will bring the biggest crisis the modern world will ever see.

Problems Require Solutions

What is our goal?

Our goal is to mitigate the negative impact of globalization while still deriving the benefits it brings to the people. These goals include:

  1. Raising worker standards all around the world. This will be achieved once all the nations are visibly implementing and actively enforcing worker policies that follow the Universal Declaration of Human Rights.
  2. Combat Climate Change. Through the development of green alternatives and the discouragement of fossil fuel, our goal is for the world to meet the goal that the Paris agreement set in 2015. This is to reduce the temperature climb from 2°C to 1.5°C.
  3. Protect the environment. Protection from privatization and irreversible damages will allow sustainable prosperity. Reduce deforestation by 25% by 2030 and crackdown on illegal deforestation. Clean up the oceans, revive 10% of the coral reefs and remove all visible garbage off the beaches and shores.
  4. Ensure Transnational Corporations (TMC's) are contributing to sustainable prosperity. Our goal is to create an international standard for TMC's that promote environmental preservation and ethical practices. All TMC's operating outside their home country will have to abide by both the new standard and local laws where they operate in.
  5. Compact wealth inequality. Help developing countries with raising minimum wages and developing infrastructure. The goal is to lower the poverty line, 10-30%, in ten years. Another goal is to encourage progressive tax systems so everyone pays their fair shares, a percentage of their total income.

The Proposal

  • Create a international workers committee that will help fund and enforce better worker standards around the world where it is lacking.
  • Give out funds and incentives to groups working towards cleaning up the earth and counteracting global warming. Provide education, (advertisement, videos, presentations), to help educate people on the issue.
  • Encourage countries to develop laws that promote sustainable prosperity for both corporations and the surrounding environment.
  • Require Transnational Corporations to follow an international standard inline to the Paris agreement limiting how many natural resources, (minerals, trees, the ocean), they can mine within a certain period. Simultaneously, force TMC's to follow regulations regarding how they can extract these resources in a more eco-friendly way.
  • Give out funds and incentives to countries, particularly developing nations, to increase the development of basic infrastructure and raise the standard of living. Encourage countries to adopt a progressive tax system that taxes citizens fairly based on their wealth.
Challenges and future endeavors

How are we going to achieve this?

We plan to network with numerous organizations and any reputable charities who will help us achieve our goals. We hope to collaborate with the WTO in creating "fairer" free trade for all parties, by giving everyone a voice and equal amounts of power in the matter. Along with this mitigating the negative impacts of economic globalization through preemptive action. We would then like to team up with the UN itself to promote environmental sustainability, using their influence to encourage more eco-friendly behavior.

What we face and what to expect

It's a guarantee that we will face resistance on our journey. The most likely source will be transnational corporations that are currently benefiting from the WTO and emits a lot of pollution. We plan to work with these companies to find a middle ground everyone will be happy with. With slow, gradual progress, this will allow the world to adapt at a reasonable pace. Once the action is in motion, it will also address the issue of getting people on our side. Momentum needs to build up, we give the first push.

The Future

How we will change the world

When the world successfully fulfills this proposal, we will have succeeded in promoting sustainable prosperity. While economic globalization may initially be slowed down, better workers and a healthy environment will benefit everyone. Life should not have a price tag. Reducing our consumption of natural resources will allow for future sustainability, and ethical working standards will eliminate exploitation through things like outsourcing and sweatshops. In all, the passing of this proposal to the UN will be a win to humanity, for us and the future generations.
